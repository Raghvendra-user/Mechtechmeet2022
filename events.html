Build a bridge entirely made up of Popsicle sticks with optimum efficiency.

The decision of judges shall be final and no arguments or appeal will be entairtained.

  • Rules
    1. . Loading will be done at the centre of the bridge. (on the top)
    2. All construction and material requirements will be checked prior to testing. Bridges failing to meet these requirements will be disqualified.
    3. Central deflection up to 15 mm is tolerable. Sinking of supports at the end will lead to disqualification
    4. Loading will be done until the limit of deflection is reached or breaking of truss/ span members occurs
    5. The bridge with highest structural efficiency (E) will be declared as winner. E = load supported in kg/ weight of the bridge in kg
    6. The top two teams will be awarded.
    7. . All the dimensions mentioned are acceptable within an error range of +/- 5%. Any failure in adhering to the specifications will lead to immediate disqualification.
    8. All decisions of the judges shall be final.
  • Fees
    1. Entry fees : 200/-
  • Bridge Specifications
    1. Construct a bridge of length (span) at least 500mm and up to 800 mm using standard Popsicle sticks and adhesives.
    2. The bridge cannot have more than two footings
    3. Overlap criteria -The structure must be a truss (not a beam), not more than three Popsiclesticks should be overlapped to make a truss member except at the joint
    4. Footing cannot be created by an overlapping network of sticks
    5. The width of the bridge at any point shall not be more than 150mm.
    6. Total height of the bridge from footing must not be less than 150mm and must not exceed 300mm.
    7. . Loading will be done at the center of the bridge. (On the top)
    8. At starting a load of 20 kg. will be put on your bridge for 30 sec. to check the stability of bridge
    9. After that, loading will be done with a rate of 5 kg. /10 sec.
  • Materials Specifications
    1. The bridge must be constructed only from Popsicle sticks (120mm x 12mm x 2mm) and adhesives (Synthetic white adhesive or single component cyanoacrylate adhesive eg. FEVICOL and glue sticks).
    2. No other materials may be used. The bridge may not be stained, painted or coated in any fashion with any foreign substance.
    3. The Popsicle sticks may be cut or trimmed to any shape or size.
    4. . The adhesive can only be used to join Popsicle sticks together; however, adhesives cannot be applied on the free surface of the member sticks to increase its strength.
  • A team can have maximum of 4 members.
  • Students from different semesters, branches and college may participate together as a team.

The bots shall pop out the balloons. Each balloon will represent 1 life. Each bot has to knock out its opponent (by popping out the balloons).

The decision of judges shall be final and no arguments or appeal will be entairtained.

  • Rules for Round 1
    1. The bot shall have attachment points for two balloon strings where each balloon indicates 1 life. The bot with highest number of lives/balloons remaining at the end of round within stipulated time will be declared as the winner
    2. The pin to burst the balloon on the bot should not be more than “4cm off-bot”.
    3. Use of any hot wire/electric means for bursting balloons is not permitted.
    4. Each bot will have 4 lives (i.e 4 balloons, 2 attached at any instant)
    5. Each clash will be of 4 minutes.
    6. In case of tie, extra time will be provided until the next balloon pops
  • Rules for Round 2
    1. The bots will physically clash against each other, where certain points shall be awarded according to the following scheme,
    2. 5 points: For each time a bot corners the opponent, hence immobilizing its further movements.
    3. 10 points: For each time, a bot flips the opponent upside down.
  • Fees
    1. Entry fees : 200/-
  • Victory Criteria
    1. Same opponents matched will play both the rounds.
    2. If a bot breaks down the opponent bot in round 2, in such a case the bot shall be declared as the winner of the Robo War. (Regardless of Points scored)
  • The dimension of robot should not exceed 25x25x20 cm3 (L*B*H) however tolerance of 10% will allowed.
  • The weight of bot shall not exceed 20 Kilograms.
  • The potential difference between any two points on robot must not exceed 24 Volts (no current limit).
  • Both wired & wireless robots are allowed.
  • The bots can have either onboard or off board batteries or AC adaptors.
  • Participants with wired robots are strictly advised to get wire of length 5 m or more and the wire should remain slack under all circumstances during the competition.

To score maximum goals and to defend your own goal post in the match.

The decision of judges shall be final and no arguments or appeal will be entairtained.

  • Rules
    1. 90 seconds of setup time will be provided to each team for pre-game setup and testing.
    2. Fixtures will be designed by match referee.
    3. Each match will be a KNOCKOUT match. Each match will consist of 2 halves of 2.5 minutes (150 seconds).Half time provided between two halves will be of 1 minute.
    4. Winning team will qualify for the next round. Total number of rounds will depend upon total number of participating teams.
    5. Game will start by placing the ball at the centre and bots out of the centre circle in their respective halves.
    6. If there is entanglement between the wires of the bots then match will be restored from the centre without stopping the time.
    7. After the first half goals will be swapped.
    8. In case of a tie, the winner will be decided via golden goal rule.
    9. If the ball completely crosses the line then only the goal will be considered.
    10. Team who scores the maximum goals would be the winner of the match.
    11. In case of odd number of participating teams the first even set of teams will play matches and the later coming team will be put on hold and the loser with the least goal difference will be given the chance to play a knockout match with the on hold team.
    12. If two or more team losses with the same goal difference then the losing team that scores more number of goals will go further to play the knockout match the on hold team.
    13. If the score line of two losing teams is same then the time for their first goal will be recorded and the team with least first goal time will play the knock out match.
  • Fees
    1. Entry fees : 200/-
  • Each team is allowed to have only one bot.
  • Table tennis ball will be provided for the game play.
  • The bot must fit into a cube of (30*30*30) cm all the time. It may not expand at any time during the match beyond this size even for hitting the ball. Violating this will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • The bot should be controlled manually using wired or wireless remote control.
  • The bots can have on board well as off board battery or AC adaptors.
  • Teams will be supplied 220v, 50Hz AC supply only.
  • The maximum potential difference b/w any two points on the bot should not exceed 24 volts.
  • Flippers or dozers can be used in bots with suitable mechanism so that not to damage the arena.
  • Maximum weight of the bot is 10 Kg (including battery or adaptor, if on-board)
  • 10% tolerance allowed in any one dimension.
  • Grabbing the ball is not allowed. So bots should not have any kind of grabbing mechanism.
  • A team can have maximum of 3 members.
  • Students from different semesters, branches and college may participate together as a team.
